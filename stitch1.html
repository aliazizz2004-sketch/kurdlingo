ephemeral

/ɪˈfɛmərəl/
adjective
1

Lasting for a very short time.

"The ephemeral joys of childhood are often the ones we miss the most."
2

(of plants) Having a very short life cycle, typically completing its life in a single season.

"The desert floor was briefly covered with ephemeral blooms after the rare rainfall."

link Synonyms & Antonyms

Synonyms

Antonyms

history_edu Etymology

Late 16th century: from Greek ephēmeros ‘lasting only a day’, from epi- ‘upon’ + hēmera ‘day’.
